title: "Learning to Uncommit"
date: 2026-06-25
---
One of the hardest lessons in software engineering—and perhaps in life—is learning when to throw work away. 

We suffer from the sunk cost fallacy. We spend two weeks building a feature, realize the architecture is fundamentally flawed, and instead of starting over, we patch it. We add duct tape. We add technical debt.

### The Power of `git reset --hard`
There is incredible power in recognizing a dead end and having the courage to reverse course. Uncommitting isn't admitting defeat; it's recognizing that the first attempt was just a prototype. It was the process of learning *how* the thing should actually be built.

When you learn to uncommit, you free yourself from the burden of bad past decisions. You allow your software to evolve based on what you know today, rather than being constrained by what you didn't know yesterday. 

Next time you find yourself fighting against your own codebase, take a breath. Step back. And don't be afraid to drop the branch and start fresh.
