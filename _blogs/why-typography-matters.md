We spend the majority of our waking hours reading screens. From urgent emails to long-form essays, typography is the voice in which the internet speaks to us. 

Yet, it is often an afterthought in the development process. 

### The Psychology of Fonts
Different typefaces carry different emotional weights. A geometric sans-serif feels objective and modern, while a humanist serif feels warm, authoritative, and deeply personal. When building a platform—especially a blog—the font you choose dictates the tone of every word written on it.

### The Technical Execution
But good typography isn't just about picking a nice font from Google Fonts. It's about the math of reading:
1.  **Line Height (Leading):** A line height of `1.6` to `1.8` provides enough breathing room for the eye to reset on the next line without getting lost.
2.  **Line Length (Measure):** The optimal line length is between 50 and 75 characters. Any longer, and the eye struggles to track back to the beginning of the next line.
3.  **Contrast:** True black `#000000` on pure white `#FFFFFF` can cause eye strain due to the stark contrast. Softening the text to `#11100E` and the background to `#F8F1E5` creates a much more pleasant reading experience.

When typography is executed perfectly, it becomes invisible. The reader stops seeing letters and starts seeing ideas.
